## Challenge

This project involved me constructing and coding a smart mirror using an old computer monitor, an acrylic panel + two way mirror film, and other parts. The tutorial I used for the frame was <a href="http://www.whatimade.today/easily-build-a-magic-mirror-with-a-raspberry-pi-and-standard-50x50-cm-ikea-frame/"> this</a>, and <a href="https://magicmirror.builders/"> here</a> is where to find MagicMirror<sup>2</sup>, the open source modular smart mirror platform.

I was super lucky and found a used Rasberry Pi on my campus marketplace, and the original monitor that I used I received for free before switching it out with the PLANAR. With IKEA located right next to my apartment, I was able to save on shipping costs as well. I modified the weather display to remind me to grab an umbrella when its raining, and I implemented a module that shows upcoming movies with their Rotten Tomato score. I wanted to customize it even more, so I developed and added a module one that displays a quote I like, with a new one every 2 min.


## Takeaways

This project seriously made me realize the importance of and joy in doing things outside of your schoolwork, and I'm really happy with how it turned out. It was my first time contributing to an open source project, and I will definitely continue to do so in the future. Since building it the first time, I sought where to make adjustments and iterated - replaced the monitor with one that fit against the frame more, and I'm planning on more improvements in the future, like possibly a camera.
